Research

Research Overview

Exploring the frontiers of intelligent systems and analytics to solve complex problems

The Laboratory for Intelligent Systems and Analytics (LISA) conducts cutting-edge research at the intersection of artificial intelligence, machine learning, complex systems analysis, and their applications in manufacturing, healthcare, and supply chain management.

Our interdisciplinary approach combines theoretical foundations with practical applications, leveraging expertise from various domains to develop innovative solutions to complex real-world problems.

We collaborate with academic partners, industry stakeholders, and government agencies to ensure our research has meaningful impact and addresses pressing challenges in society and industry.

Research Areas

Our lab focuses on several key research areas

AI & ML in Manufacturing and Healthcare

Our research in AI and machine learning focuses on developing and applying advanced techniques to solve complex problems in manufacturing processes and healthcare systems. We work on:

  • Deep learning for predictive maintenance and quality control
  • Reinforcement learning for process optimization
  • Computer vision for defect detection and medical image analysis
  • Natural language processing for medical records analysis
  • Explainable AI for critical decision-making contexts

Our goal is to create AI systems that not only perform well but are also interpretable, robust, and can be deployed in real-world manufacturing and healthcare environments.

Complex Networks

Our complex networks research focuses on analyzing and modeling systems that can be represented as networks of interconnected components. We investigate:

  • Network structure and dynamics in social, biological, and technological systems
  • Community detection and network clustering algorithms
  • Temporal network analysis for evolving systems
  • Network resilience and robustness under perturbations
  • Graph neural networks for learning on network-structured data

This research provides insights into how complex systems function, how information or diseases spread, and how to design more resilient networked systems.

Smart Manufacturing

Our smart manufacturing research aims to advance the state-of-the-art in manufacturing through data-driven approaches, IoT integration, and intelligent decision-making systems. We focus on:

  • Digital twins for manufacturing processes and systems
  • IoT and sensor networks for real-time monitoring
  • Cyber-physical systems for advanced manufacturing
  • Predictive maintenance and quality control
  • Human-machine collaboration in smart factories

This research contributes to the development of more efficient, flexible, and sustainable manufacturing systems that can adapt to changing conditions and requirements.

Supply Chain Resilience

Our supply chain resilience research focuses on developing models and methodologies to enhance the resilience and efficiency of supply chain networks in the face of disruptions. We investigate:

  • Network-based models of supply chain systems
  • Risk assessment and mitigation strategies
  • Real-time monitoring and early warning systems
  • Adaptive and reconfigurable supply chain designs
  • Multi-objective optimization for balancing efficiency and resilience

This research helps organizations design and manage supply chains that can withstand disruptions while maintaining operational efficiency and meeting customer demands.

Current Projects

Ongoing research initiatives at our laboratory

AI-Driven Predictive Maintenance for Manufacturing Systems

2023-2026

This project develops advanced AI algorithms for predictive maintenance in manufacturing environments, leveraging sensor data and machine learning to predict equipment failures before they occur.

Funded by: National Science Foundation

Network-Based Analysis of Healthcare Systems

2024-2027

This project applies complex network theory to model and analyze healthcare systems, with the goal of improving patient flow, resource allocation, and overall system efficiency.

Funded by: National Institutes of Health

Digital Twin Framework for Smart Manufacturing

2022-2025

This project develops a comprehensive digital twin framework for smart manufacturing, integrating real-time data with simulation models to enable dynamic decision-making and process optimization.

Funded by: Department of Energy

Resilient Supply Chain Design Under Uncertainty

2023-2026

This project develops models and methodologies for designing resilient supply chains that can withstand various types of disruptions while maintaining operational efficiency.

Funded by: Industry Partners

Research Facilities

State-of-the-art facilities supporting our research activities

Computing Infrastructure

Our lab is equipped with high-performance computing resources, including GPU clusters for deep learning research, distributed computing systems for large-scale data processing, and specialized hardware for real-time analytics.

Data Analytics Laboratory

Our data analytics laboratory provides tools and software for data collection, processing, visualization, and analysis. It supports research in machine learning, statistical modeling, and data mining.

Smart Manufacturing Testbed

Our smart manufacturing testbed includes miniaturized manufacturing equipment, IoT devices, sensors, and control systems that allow researchers to experiment with new manufacturing paradigms in a controlled environment.

Collaborative Research Space

Our collaborative research space is designed to facilitate interaction and knowledge sharing among researchers, with meeting rooms, whiteboards, and presentation equipment for discussions and brainstorming sessions.
